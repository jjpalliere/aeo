The ecosystem around your brand

Your brand is the sum of its online presence — every page, mention, review, and citation that AI models draw from when answering questions about your category.

AEO submits prompts to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini via API and analyzes hundreds of responses: which brands get mentioned, in what rank order, which URLs get cited. At that volume, consistent patterns emerge — a map of how the AI ecosystem sees you relative to everyone else.

Addressing the demands of every ideal customer profile

A CFO evaluating a purchase and the practitioner who'd use the product day-to-day are asking fundamentally different questions. Each has separate interactions with LLMs, different competitors surfacing, different sources cited.

Every prompt is mapped to a specific ideal customer profile. We use different forms of data — funnel stage, intent signals, role context — to make sure we're accurately capturing what each persona needs and where the brand is falling short.

How audiences engage across different surfaces

Audiences engage in unique ways across different channels — search, AI chat, review platforms, industry publications. How you show up on each determines whether you reach them at all.

The channel analysis classifies every cited URL: owned pages, competitor pages, news outlets, review platforms. Outlets that consistently appear but haven't covered your brand are PR targets. Sources that surface for competitors but not for you show where distribution is thin. How you engage alongside other players on each channel matters as much as whether you're present.

Mapping formats to channels to personas

This isn't just about discoverability. It's about exposing your brand to the different digital mediators — whether that's search engines, LLMs, or aggregator platforms — in the formats they can actually parse and surface.

We map which content formats perform on which channels for which personas. The goal is to understand not just where to be, but what shape the content needs to take to reach the right people through the right surface.

Competitive opportunities and blue sky territory

The gap analysis surfaces what competitors are doing well that you should draw from, what they're neglecting that you can own, and where neither side is playing yet.

Part of this is making sure you're present in the same spaces as your competitors — signaling to AI that you're a legitimate, active authority in the category. The other part is finding blue sky territory: topics and angles nobody has claimed, where being first means owning the conversation.
