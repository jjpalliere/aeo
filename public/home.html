Strategy Brief
92% of AI-summarized searches never result in a click.
LLMs are being asked questions about your brand every day. AEO is how you understand what they're saying — and influence it.
Visibility
Querying at scale

There's no platform that shows how an LLM represents a brand. Responses vary — the same question asked twice returns a different answer.

The tool submits prompts to each LLM via API and analyzes hundreds of responses: which brands get mentioned, in what rank order, which URLs get cited. At volume, patterns emerge.

Personas
Scored per buyer type

A CFO evaluating a purchase asks completely different questions than the team member who'd use the product day-to-day.

Every prompt is mapped to a persona. Performance is scored at the prompt level, then rolled up: where a brand is well-represented and where it's absent.

Channels
Third-party sources carry more weight than owned pages

The channel analysis classifies every cited URL: owned pages, competitor pages, news outlets, industry sites, review platforms.

Outlets that appear consistently but haven't covered a brand are PR targets. Sources that surface for competitors but not for a brand show where distribution is thin.

Format
Structure determines whether LLMs can use content

Headers as questions create direct query matches. FAQ sections mirror the format LLMs use in responses. Schema markup gives the AI an immediate read on what a page is about.

Gaps
What a brand covers vs. what buyers actually ask

A website reflects what a brand has decided to talk about. Buyers ask LLMs who the main players are, how pricing works, and how approaches compare.

The gap analysis maps three things against each other. Where they don't overlap is where content strategy has room to grow.
